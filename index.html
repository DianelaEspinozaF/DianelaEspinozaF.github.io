About Me

Hi! I'm Dianela, an economist from Chiclayo, Peru, currently pursuing a Master's in Data Science and Public Policy at the London School of Economics (LSE) as a Winner of Peruvian Scholarship for Public Service.

My research focuses on using rigorous quantitative methods to evaluate social programs and understand educational inequalities. I'm particularly interested in education, early childhood development, conditional cash transfers, and the role of data science in informing evidence-based policy in developing countries.

Before LSE, I worked as a Research Assistant at GRADE on the Young Lives longitudinal study (Ni√±os del Milenio in Peru), and as a Research Intern at Innovations for Poverty Action, where I contributed to projects on gender gaps in STEM and intercultural bilingual education.

Research in Progress

Long-term Effects of Conditional Cash Transfers on Human Capital and Family Formation

with Alan Sanchez (University of Oxford) and Amanda Woodman (University of Oxford)

I evaluate whether Peru's conditional cash transfer programme (JUNTOS) impacts long-term human capital and family formation outcomes using propensity score matching on longitudinal data (2006-2023) from 2,000+ Young Lives participants.
